## Problem

This problem comes from [Project Euler 29](https://projecteuler.net/problem=29)

<div class="max-md:hidden">

> **Problem**
> 
> Consider all integer combinations of $$ a^b$$ for $$ 2 \leq a \leq 5$$ and $$ 2 \leq b \leq 5$$:
> $$ \begin{matrix} 2^2=4, &2^3=8, &2^4=16, &2^5=32\\ 3^2=9, &3^3=27, &3^4=81, &3^5=243\\ 4^2=16, &4^3=64, &4^4=256, &4^5=1024\\ 5^2=25, &5^3=125, &5^4=625, &5^5=3125 \end{matrix}$$ 
> 
> If they are then placed in numerical order, with any repeats removed, we get the following sequence of 15 distinct terms:
>
> 4, 8, 9, 16, 25, 27, 32, 64, 81, 125, 243, 256, 625, 1024, 3125.
>
> How many distinct terms are in the sequence generated by $$ a^b$$ for $$ 2 \leq a \leq 100$$ and $$ 2 \leq b \leq 100$$?

</div>

## Solution
I decided to go with the most straight-forward solution.
1. For $$ 2 \leq a \leq 100$$ and $$ 2 \leq b \leq 100$$, calculate $$ a^b$$ 
2. Put the result into a `set`, so that it automatically removes duplicates
3. Print the length of the `set`

## Code
<Tabs>
<Tab name="problem29.py" checked="checked">
```python
# Project Euler: Problem 29
# Distinct Powers

ab = set()

for a in range(2, 101):
    for b in range(2, 101):
        ab.add(a**b)

print(len(ab))
```
